How your privacy is protected

Your passcode never leaves your browser in readable form — not at signup, not at login, not during a passcode reset. The server only ever sees a one-way PBKDF2 verifier of it, the same way it's stored, so there is nothing "real" for anyone with server or database access to steal. Your actual encryption key is derived locally and used to encrypt/decrypt entries in this browser only.

The server does run a background clock that deletes each entry's ciphertext the moment it expires, whether or not anyone is logged in — that's what makes the countdown trustworthy even with the app closed. But because the server never holds your key, a background deletion can only produce a generic notification (mode + rough length, no words). Rich notifications with the real text or an exact redacted replay only happen when this app was open at the moment an entry expired, since that's the only time your key exists anywhere outside your own head. Turn off "Include entry text" above if you'd rather those rich notifications never include your actual words either.

Admins can see your username, email address, and signup date, and can trigger a passcode reset — but the new passcode itself is generated by the server and emailed directly to you, never seen or typed by an admin. No admin action, under any circumstance, exposes your entries or your passcode. Every time the account list is viewed, that's logged to the audit trail.

Delete my account and data

This permanently deletes your account, your email address, any entries still counting down, and your notification history. It cannot be undone — there is no backup copy anywhere, by design.
